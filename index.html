Millions of Americans still don't have internet access, according to a study by the FCC

By John Honan

We’ve all experienced a slow or weak internet connection. You're in the middle of a 3 hour online class or an important meeting on Zoom when the dreaded “Your internet connection is unstable” message pops up. You drop from the meeting and have to email your professor or boss to let him know your internet sucks. Yes, this exact scenario happened to me.

As familiar as this scenario may be to students and remote workers across America, many families are dealing with far worse internet connections. Some have no internet in their area at all. The FCC reported that 21.3 million Americans lack access to broadband internet, according to Broadband Now.

Each person’s ideal internet speed is determined by their needs. For homes with 1-2 devices connected to the same internet connection, up to 25 mpbs should be fast enough to handle browsing Wikipedia, Instagram, send emails, and stream videos, according to Tom’s Guide. Anywhere from 50-100 mpbs will do the job for households with 3-5 devices using the home’s internet, while households with more than 5 devices will require 150 to 200 mpbs. Families in situation 1 will probably most likely only need to pay for a DSL subscription which needs about 6 mpbs. Cable would be ideal for 3-5 device families and a fiber connection would work best for households with more than 5 devices.

Comparing internet prices by state
